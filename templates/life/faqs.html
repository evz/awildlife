Wild Foods FAQs

How do you know what plants are safe to eat?

This subject is the first thing to be discussed on plant walks and yard consultations. Eating wild foods can be as safe as it is exciting when you know what you are doing. I will teach people the protocol around how to identify wild edibles and how to forage responsibly.

What are the benefits of eating wild foods?

Wild food is growing all around you, in the cracks of the sidewalks, in the shade of a tree in someone’s back yard. Often time the weeds we curse at for existing are actually nutritious food: dandelions, plantain, creeping charlie and lamb’s quarters just to name a few. These wild foods have not had a human hand to protect and cultivate them, they have the ability to grow and thrive on their own and this means they have more nutrients than store bought or even farm stand vegetables.

Movement class FAQs

Why are the classes called movement classes instead of exercise classes?

Exercise is great but it’s only a small part of what it means to move your body. To quote biomechanist, Katy Bowman: “Every single thing our bodies do require movement-initiated by our own musculoskeletal system-to be performed with ease. Digestion, immunity, reproduction-all of these functions require us to move. You can eat the perfect diet, sleep get hours a night, and use only baking soda and vinegar to clean your house, but without loads created by natural movement, all of these worthy efforts are thwarted on a cellular level, and your optimal wellness level remains elusive.”

The idea behind the movement class are to get your mind and body out of the tediousness task of “exercise” and into a place where you move often and enjoy doing it. The movement classes are not repetitive. One week you might find yourself crawling and balancing on boards and the next week you work on jumping and lifting/carrying various objects. The idea is to keep it random to avoid overtraining in one particular type of movement and it also keeps boredom at bay.

Are these movement classes right for me?

The classes are designed to meet people where they are physically and emotionally. There are many progressions a person can move through at their own pace. The classes will challenge you to move your body in new ways and change the way you think about moving but never to push you into something you aren’t ready for.

Are you going to make me climb trees and carry logs?

Not unless you want to. Not until you are ready.

What should I wear to the classes?

Comfortable clothes that you can move in and don’t mind getting dirty. Outdoor classes are year round, so dress for the weather.
